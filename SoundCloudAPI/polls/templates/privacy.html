Overview

This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have. We do not sell your personal data.

1) Information we collect

  • Account data: username, email, and basic profile fields you submit.
  • Authentication data: session identifiers and security metadata (e.g., CSRF token).
  • SoundCloud data: if you connect SoundCloud, we store OAuth tokens and basic account identifiers.
  • Usage logs: basic logs (time, endpoint, errors) used for reliability and security.

2) How we use information

  • To provide login, account management, and core app features.
  • To connect to SoundCloud on your behalf (searching, fetching tracks, etc.).
  • To secure the Service, prevent abuse, and debug issues.

3) SoundCloud OAuth tokens

If you connect SoundCloud, we store access/refresh tokens server-side to make authorized API calls for you. You can disconnect SoundCloud in your account settings, which removes stored tokens from our database.

4) Sharing

  • We share data only as needed to operate the Service (e.g., hosting providers) or if required by law.
  • When you use SoundCloud features, your requests interact with SoundCloud’s systems under their policies.
  • We do not sell personal data.

5) Cookies & sessions

We use cookies/session storage for authentication and security. Disabling cookies may prevent login from working.

6) Data retention

We retain account data while your account is active. If you delete your account, we remove or anonymize your data, except where retention is required for legal or security purposes.

7) Security

We use reasonable safeguards to protect data, but no system is 100% secure. Keep your password safe and use strong credentials.

8) Your choices

  • Access/update your account information from your profile/settings.
  • Disconnect SoundCloud to revoke the Service’s ability to access SoundCloud on your behalf.
  • Request account deletion through support.

9) Changes

We may update this policy. We’ll update the “Last updated” date above when changes are published.
